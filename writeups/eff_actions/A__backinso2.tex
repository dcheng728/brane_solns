\section{Back to SO(2)}
The complete 4-point effective action is already known, but it is commonly written in U(1) covariant form, which makes the 2x2 torus metric a little obscure, so we may try to put it back to SO(2) form.

So here is the strategy:
\begin{itemize}
    \item write everything in the known 4-pt effective action in SO(2) rather than U(1), as in with $F_3, H_4, \tau_1, \tau_2$ rather than $G_3, \mathcal{P}_m, Q_m$; We don't need to use $C, e^{-\Phi}$ because we are already familiar with the metric with $\tau_1, \tau_2$ in SO(2) form.
    \item substitute in metric on the 2x2 torus $M^{ab}$ for $\tau_1, \tau_2$. Note that $M^{11} = \frac{|\tau|^2}{\tau_2}, M^{12} = -\frac{\tau_1}{\tau_2}, M^{22} = \frac{1}{\tau_2}$.
    \item see if there is a 12d interpretation
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Riemannn tensor}

Given this metric, with the 2x2 torus having indices 10, 11, we compute all nonvanishing components of the Riemann tensor

\begin{equation}
\mathcal{G}_{MN}
= \begin{pmatrix}
    g_{mn} & 0 & 0 \\
    0 & \frac{1}{\tau_2} & \frac{\tau_1}{\tau_2} \\
    0 & \frac{\tau_1}{\tau_2} & \frac{|\tau|^2}{\tau_2}
\end{pmatrix}
\end{equation}

We have that
\begin{equation}
g_{ab} = \frac{1}{\tau_2}\begin{pmatrix}
    1 & \tau_1 \\
    \tau_1 & |\tau|^2
\end{pmatrix}
\end{equation}

From this 12d metric, one immediately finds that the nonvanishing components of the Riemann tensor are $\hat{\Gamma}^p_{mn} = \Gamma^p{}_{mn}$, $\hat{\Gamma}^a{}_{bm} = \frac{1}{2}g^{ac}g_{bc,m}$, $\hat{\Gamma}^m{}_{ab} = - \frac{1}{2}g^{mp}g_{ab,p}$.
And we evaluate that $a$ for rows and $b$ for columns, we have
\begin{equation}

\end{equation}
