Magnetic ansatz

From the form field identities given in conventions.md, one can show a relation between the form field and its Hodge dual

SMN∣∗F=−(−1)[t]SMN∣F. S_{MN}|_{*F} = -(-1)^{[t]}S_{MN}|_{F}. SMN​∣∗F​=−(−1)[t]SMN​∣F​.

So for spacetime with an odd number of timelike directions, the Hodge dual of the electric solution is the magnetic solution. Performing the duality transformation, we find that for a D−d−1D-d-1D−d−1 form ∗F*F∗F, it supports the d−1d-1d−1 brane ansatz with

(∗F)m1...mD−d−1=−cεm1...mD−d−1m∂mH. (* F)_{m_1... m_{D-d-1}} = - c\varepsilon_{m_1...m_{D-d-1} m}\partial_m H. (∗F)m1​...mD−d−1​​=−cεm1​...mD−d−1​m​∂m​H.

From this we conclude that a (d−1)(d-1)(d−1)-brane can either be supported by a (d+1)(d+1)(d+1)-form or a (D−d−1)(D-d-1)(D−d−1)-form.

12d to 10d reduction ansatz

What should be the reduction ansatz from 12 to 10? In 12d one should not know about the torus, hence know nothing about 10d. Only once the torus is introduced the 10d theory is obtained via 12 = 10 + 2. The reduction ansatz should only break symmetry as 12 = 10 + 2.

ds122=Hfds22+Hgds102 ds_{12}^2 = H^f ds_2^2 + H^g ds_{10}^2 ds122​=Hfds22​+Hgds102​
ds122=Hfds22+Hg−12ds1,32+Hg+12ds62 ds_{12}^2 = H^f ds_2^2 + H^{g-\frac{1}{2}}ds_{1,3}^2 + H^{g+\frac{1}{2}}ds_6^2 ds122​=Hfds22​+Hg−21​ds1,32​+Hg+21​ds62​

Then either (1) the reduction came from 12d 5-brane, f=g−12f=g-\frac{1}{2}f=g−21​ or (2) the reduction came from 12d 3-brane f=g+12f=g+\frac{1}{2}f=g+21​.

12d 5-brane

ds122=Hfds1,52+Hf+1ds62 ds_{12}^2 = H^f ds_{1,5}^2 + H^{f+1}ds_6^2 ds122​=Hfds1,52​+Hf+1ds62​

Solving using above formula yields

ds122=H−25ds1,52+H35ds62 ds_{12}^2 = H^{-\frac{2}{5}}ds_{1,5}^2 +H^{\frac{3}{5}}ds_6^2 ds122​=H−52​ds1,52​+H53​ds62​

12d 3-brane

ds122=Hf−1ds1,32+Hfds82 ds_{12}^2 = H^{f-1} ds_{1,3}^2 + H^{f}ds_8^2 ds122​=Hf−1ds1,32​+Hfds82​

Solving using above formula yields

This makes sense; try to balance with this.

Alternative

Following Kelly's paper, you shall find 12d metrics balanced by 5 and 7-forms

ds122=H−1/2ds1,32+H2/3ds82 ds_{12}^2 = H^{-1/2}ds_{1,3}^2 + H^{2/3}ds_8^2 ds122​=H−1/2ds1,32​+H2/3ds82​

But if you wanted to reduce these to 10d and obtain the D3 solution, the 12 to 10 ansatz is not in 12=2+10 form. So perhaps you should not try to follow this.
