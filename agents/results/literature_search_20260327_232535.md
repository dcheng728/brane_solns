### 1. Self-Dual Form Fields and Democratic Formulations (Section 6)

---

**[A] Mkrtchyan & Valach, "Democratic actions for type II supergravities"**
- arXiv: `2207.00626` | Phys. Rev. D **107** (2023) 066027

*Summary:* Constructs a universal democratic Lagrangian for the bosonic sector of both type IIA and IIB supergravities, treating electric and magnetic RR potentials on equal footing. For type IIB, the self-duality equation of the 5-form is derived from the Lagrangian (rather than imposed by hand). Also presents an alternative IIB form with manifest SL(2,R) symmetry.

*Relevance:* Directly extends the self-dual 5-form discussion in Section 6, where the 12d Hodge duality is used to implement the 10d self-duality condition. The manifest SL(2,R) formulation is directly relevant to the duality discussion throughout.

*Relation to review:* **Extends** — provides a concrete Lagrangian realization of the democratic formulation the review relies on.

---

**[B] Hull, "Covariant Action for Self-Dual p-Form Gauge Fields in General Spacetimes"**
- arXiv: `2307.04748` | JHEP **04** (2024) 011

*Summary:* A natural generalization of Sen's action in which the auxiliary Minkowski metric is replaced by a second dynamical metric. The theory is fully covariant and formulated on any spacetime. The physical sector is a chiral p-form coupled to the metric; there is a decoupled shadow sector. Addresses covariance issues in Sen's original construction.

*Relevance:* Directly relevant to Section 6's use of Sen's formalism for the type IIB 5-form. The shadow sector structure could have implications for any 12d action ansatz that uses a Sen-type action for the 5-form.

*Relation to review:* **Extends** — provides the most complete covariant framework for Sen's approach, which underpins Section 6.

---

**[C] Vanichchapongjaroen, "Duality-symmetric D3-brane action with twisted self-dual 2-form doublet"**
- arXiv: `2506.07219` | JHEP **10** (2025) 223

*Summary:* Applies Sen's formalism to construct an explicit SL(2,R) duality-symmetric D3-brane action coupled to the full type IIB supergravity background. The worldvolume contains a doublet of 2-form fields satisfying a twisted self-duality condition. Proves diffeomorphism and kappa symmetry.

*Relevance:* Very directly relevant to Section 5 on the D3-brane and to the review's central claim that the D3-brane's worldvolume SL(2,Z) duality is intimately linked to the bulk type IIB S-duality. This paper gives a complete covariant action that makes the worldvolume SL(2,R) manifest.

*Relation to review:* **Extends** — makes the D3 worldvolume SL(2,R) manifest at the action level, strengthening the review's discussion.

### 2. IKKT Matrix Model and D(-1)/pp-wave (Sections 4 and 7)

---

**[D] Ciceri & Samtleben, "Holography for the IKKT matrix model"**
- arXiv: `2503.08771` | Phys. Rev. Lett. (2025) [companion to Ciceri:2025wpb = `2511.23111`]

*Summary:* Identifies the lowest gauge-invariant operators of the IKKT model with KK fluctuations of Euclidean IIB supergravity on the D(-1) background. Constructs the bulk realization as a 1d maximal supergravity with 32 supercharges and SO(10). Crucially: constructs half-BPS solutions and **explicitly uplifts them to pp-waves in twelve dimensions**.

*Relevance:* Extremely directly relevant to the review's discussion of D(-1) as a 12d pp-wave compactification (Section 4). The 12d pp-wave uplift is a new explicit result confirming the D(-1)↔12d pp-wave connection. Note: `Ciceri:2025wpb` (2511.23111) is the *follow-up* companion paper already in the bib; this earlier letter (`2503.08771`) should also be cited.

*Relation to review:* **Directly supports and extends** one of the review's main claims.

---

**[E] Hartnoll & Liu, "Statistical Physics of the Polarised IKKT Matrix Model"**
- arXiv: `2504.06481` | SciPost Phys. **19** (2025) 099

*Summary:* Follow-up to `Hartnoll:2024csr` (already in bib). Studies the IKKT matrix integral as a statistical mechanics system as a function of the deformation parameter Ω. Finds a first-order phase transition at a critical Ω²N, corresponding to tunneling from a polarised fuzzy-sphere D1-brane configuration to a gas of well-separated D-instantons. The competing saddle is identified with spherical (p,q) 5-branes.

*Relevance:* Section 7 / IKKT discussion. The phase transition between D(-1) and D1 configurations has potential implications for understanding the moduli of the 12d pp-wave compactification.

*Relation to review:* **Extends** the IKKT program with non-perturbative structure.

### 3. Higher-Derivative Corrections and Modular Properties (Sections 6 and 7)

---

**[F] Dorigoni & Treilis, "Two string theory flavours of generalised Eisenstein series"**
- arXiv: `2307.07491` | JHEP **11** (2023) 102

*Summary:* Studies two distinct classes of generalised Eisenstein series — non-holomorphic modular functions satisfying inhomogeneous Laplace eigenvalue equations. The first class arises in the low-energy expansion of the type IIB effective action (higher-derivative coefficients). The second contains two-loop modular graph functions from genus-one amplitudes. Unifies both via a Poincaré series approach.

*Relevance:* Directly relevant to Section 6/7's discussion of modular completions and Eisenstein series in higher-derivative couplings. Provides the mathematical framework for the non-holomorphic Eisenstein series mentioned in the review.

*Relation to review:* **Supports and extends** — gives rigorous structure to the modular completions discussed in the review.

---

**[G] Dorigoni, Green & Wen, "Modular Features of Superstring Scattering Amplitudes: Generalised Eisenstein Series and Theta Lifts"**
- arXiv: `2501.03996` | Annales Henri Poincaré (2025)

*Summary:* Shows that the large-N expansion of integrated 4-point correlators in N=4 SYM (and hence IIB string theory coefficients) are rational sums of real-analytic and generalised Eisenstein series. Provides a unifying theta-lift description that connects integrated correlators to two-loop modular graph functions.

*Relevance:* Connects the higher-derivative IIB effective action to N=4 SYM at finite coupling, and reinforces that modular covariance is a profound constraint. Relevant to the outlook on 5-point amplitudes.

*Relation to review:* **Extends** — broadens the case that modular structures organise IIB corrections, relevant to the 12d interpretation.

### 4. AdS/CFT and the D3-brane / M2-brane Program (Section 8 — Outlook)

---

**[H] Gautason & van Muiden, "One-loop quantization of Euclidean D3-branes in holographic backgrounds"**
- arXiv: `2402.16779` | JHEP **06** (2024) 073

*Summary:* Performs the first one-loop quantization of Euclidean D3-branes in type IIB holographic backgrounds (Euclidean AdS₅ with twisted S¹×S³ boundary). Computes the one-loop partition function and matches to non-perturbative corrections in the superconformal index of N=4 SYM at large N.

*Relevance:* Directly relevant to the outlook's question about finding the D3-brane analog of the M2-brane computation of Giombi & Tseytlin (already cited as `Giombi:2023vzu`). This paper takes a significant step in that direction — though for the superconformal index rather than the Wilson loop — and could be the starting point for the type IIB analog the review calls for.

*Relation to review:* **Extends** — provides concrete evidence that one-loop D3 computations in AdS₅×S⁵ are tractable.

---

**[I] Tseytlin et al., "Wilson loop in AdS₃×S³×T⁴ from quantum M2 brane"**
- arXiv: `2603.25590` (March 2026 — very recent)

*Summary:* Extends the M2-brane one-loop program of Giombi & Tseytlin from ABJM (AdS₄×S⁷) to the AdS₃×S³×T⁴ background. Uses a T-duality chain: type IIB on AdS₃×S³×T⁴ → type IIA near-horizon D2-D4 → M-theory on AdS₃×S³×T⁵. Computes the 1-loop M2 partition function and extracts the subleading 1/N correction.

*Relevance:* Very relevant to the outlook. The review raises the question of an AdS₅×S⁵ analog of the M2 computation; this paper shows the analogous computation works in the AdS₃ case via a similar duality chain, further motivating the AdS₅ program.

*Relation to review:* **Extends** the research program described in the Outlook, and provides guidance on the duality chain structure needed for the AdS₅ case.
