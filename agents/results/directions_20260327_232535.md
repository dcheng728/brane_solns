## 1. The 5-Point Amplitude Test: KK Loops on $T^2$ and $\varepsilon_{10}\varepsilon_{10}\mathcal{R}^4$

**Motivation:** Section 7 identifies the central limitation of the 12d effective action proposal of Minasian et al.: the $\mathfrak{t}_8\mathfrak{t}_8\mathcal{R}^4$ and $\varepsilon_{12}\varepsilon_{12}\mathcal{R}^4$ ansatz works at 4-point but fails at 5-point (Liu:2025uqu). The review argues that 4-point $t_8 t_8 R^4$ is ambiguous between a 9d$\leftrightarrow$11d and a 10d$\leftrightarrow$12d relation, since it sees only 8 transverse directions. The intrinsically 10d structure $\varepsilon_{10}\varepsilon_{10}R^4$, which first contributes at 5-point, is the discriminating observable.

**Concrete approach:**
1. Write the 5-point one-loop integrand for a massive scalar KK tower on $T^2$ using the Schwinger proper-time representation (the 5-point analogue of eq. (7.26) in the review, as given in Green:1999by and Liu:2022bfg).
2. Perform Poisson resummation on the $(m,n)$ lattice sum and extract the low-energy expansion in Mandelstam invariants.
3. Identify whether the result contains an $\varepsilon_{10}\varepsilon_{10}R^4$ contact term, and if so, whether its coefficient is the Eisenstein series $E_{3/2}(\tau,\bar\tau)$.
4. Compare with the known 10d type IIB 5-point amplitude from string theory.

**Expected outcome:** If the KK loop on $T^2$ reproduces the 5-point $\varepsilon_{10}\varepsilon_{10}R^4$ with the correct modular coefficient, this would be strong evidence that 12d $\to$ 10d is a genuine relation (not just the 11d $\to$ 9d story with a spectator). A negative result (mismatch) would sharply constrain what "12d" can mean.

**Feasibility:** Moderate (months). The 4-point Schwinger calculation is well-established; the 5-point version is technically harder but the integrand structure is known. The main challenge is the Poisson resummation and low-energy expansion at 5-point.

**References:** Green:1997as, Green:1999by, Minasian:2015bxa, Liu:2022bfg, Liu:2025uqu, Richards:0807.2421

## 2. Sen's Action and the 12d Self-Duality Problem

**Motivation:** Section 6 shows that the 10d self-duality $F_5 = *_{10}F_5$ can be recast as a 12d Hodge duality $\mathcal{F}_7 = *_{12}\mathcal{F}_5$. However, the review notes that $\tilde{F}_5$ (the composite, gauge-invariant 5-form) cannot be sensibly constructed in 12d due to rank mismatches. The 12d action (eq. 6.8) treats $\mathcal{F}_7$ as non-dynamical. Sen's covariant action for self-dual forms (Sen:1903.12196) provides a new mechanism for implementing self-duality at the action level, distinct from PST.

**Concrete approach:**
1. Write Sen's action for a self-dual 5-form in 12d (i.e., for the pair $(\mathcal{C}_4, \mathcal{C}_6)$ with $\mathcal{F}_7 = *_{12}\mathcal{F}_5$).
2. Reduce on $T^2$ using the metric ansatz $\mathcal{G}_{MN} = g_{mn} \oplus M_{ij}$ and check whether the 10d self-duality condition emerges from the 12d equations of motion.
3. Investigate whether the Chern-Simons coupling $\mathcal{C}_4 \wedge \mathcal{F}_4 \wedge \mathcal{F}_4$ can be incorporated into the Sen framework without spoiling gauge invariance.

**Expected outcome:** A 12d action from which both the 10d self-duality *and* the Chern-Simons term follow dynamically, without imposing $F_5 = *_{10}F_5$ by hand. This would elevate the 12d framework from a repackaging to a genuine parent theory (at least at two derivatives).

**Feasibility:** Moderate (months). Sen's formalism is well-developed; the main subtlety is handling the mixed Chern-Simons interactions.

**References:** Sen:1903.12196, Pasti:1996vs, DallAgata:1997gnw, Hull on democratic formulations

## 3. U(1)-Violating Terms and the 12d Obstruction

**Motivation:** Section 7 identifies U(1)-violating corrections as "a primary obstruction in finding 12d uplifts of effective actions" (citing Liu:2025uqu). The local U(1) acts on $P_m$ (charge 2) and $G_3$ (charge 1). In 12d, $\tau$ is part of the metric, so U(1) is part of the frame rotation group. U(1)-violating terms in 10d would correspond to frame-dependent terms in 12d — an apparent inconsistency.

**Concrete approach:**
1. Classify the known U(1)-violating 5-point amplitudes in type IIB (involving $G_3$ and $P_m$) and identify their modular weights.
2. Attempt to construct 12d invariants involving the 12d Riemann tensor $\mathcal{R}_{MNPQ}$ and the 4-form $\mathcal{F}_4$ that, upon torus reduction, produce objects with non-trivial U(1) charge.
3. The key insight: in 12d, the U(1) connection $Q_m = -\frac{1}{2}\partial_m \tau_1/\tau_2$ arises from the spin connection $\omega_m{}^{10,11}$. So U(1)-violating terms might correspond to 12d terms that are not invariant under local Lorentz rotations in the $(10,11)$ plane — i.e., they involve the torus vielbein explicitly.

**Expected outcome:** Either (a) a mechanism by which 12d local Lorentz invariance is broken to produce U(1)-violating 10d terms (perhaps through a topological sector or boundary effects on $T^2$), or (b) a proof that no such mechanism exists, sharpening the no-go.

**Feasibility:** Hard (months to a year). Requires careful interplay between representation theory, modular forms, and amplitude calculations.

**References:** Liu:2025uqu, Policastro:2006vt, Policastro:2008hg, Green:1997tv, Chester-Green-Pufu on modular constraints

## 4. The D3 Brane as a 12d Membrane: Resolving the Embedding Problem

**Motivation:** Section 5 identifies a tantalizing but incomplete analogy: the D2 brane dualizes to the M2 brane in 11d, suggesting the D3 should play an analogous role in 12d. But the D3's two on-shell bosonic degrees of freedom come from a gauge field $A_m$, not scalar embedding coordinates, blocking a naive uplift to a 3-brane in 12d with 2 transverse embedding scalars.

**Concrete approach:**
1. Use the PST or Sen formalism to write a manifestly SL(2,Z)-covariant D3 action where the gauge field is replaced by a pair of scalars $(y^1, y^2)$ via a self-dual decomposition. The self-duality of the D3 worldvolume field strength $F_2 = *_4 F_2$ (in 4d, the field strength of a vector is dual to another field strength) should map to the statement that $(y^1, y^2)$ parameterize the $T^2$ fiber.
2. Alternatively, consider the D3 in a background where the $T^2$ fiber degenerates (e.g., near a D7 brane). In such backgrounds, the monodromy of $\tau$ may force the gauge field to be identified with fiber coordinates.
3. Check whether the D3 worldvolume stress tensor, computed from the DBI action, matches the 12d membrane stress tensor reduced on $T^2$.

**Expected outcome:** A concrete identification of the D3 worldvolume gauge field with 12d embedding coordinates, at least in a specific class of backgrounds. This would make the D3 the genuine "12d membrane" and provide a dynamical origin for the bulk-worldvolume SL(2,Z) locking.

**Feasibility:** Hard (6-12 months). The self-dual vector/scalar duality in 4d is subtle, and the background-dependent approach adds technical complexity.

**References:** Tseytlin:1996it, Schmidhuber:1996fy, Jatkar:1996np, Kar:1997cx, Leigh:1989jq

## 5. IKKT Matrix Model and the 12d Brinkmann Geometry

**Motivation:** The appendix shows that the dual supergravity background of the mass-deformed IKKT model uplifts to a 12d Brinkmann geometry with $R_{uu} = \mu^2/2$, sourced by a 4-form flux $\mathcal{F}_4 = \mu\, du \wedge H_3$. The IKKT model is conjectured to define type IIB non-perturbatively (Ishibashi:1996xs). Recent work by Hartnoll:2024csr and Komatsu:2024bop found more general solutions with both NSNS and RR 3-forms.

**Concrete approach:**
1. Use the `src/sugra/` code to verify the Einstein equations for the general Komatsu solution uplifted to 12d. Extend the codebase to handle non-diagonal metrics of Brinkmann type.
2. Compute the 12d curvature invariants ($\mathcal{R}$, $\mathcal{R}_{MNPQ}\mathcal{R}^{MNPQ}$, etc.) and check whether the Brinkmann geometry is a pp-wave limit of a more general 12d solution (e.g., an AdS geometry).
3. Study whether the IKKT matrix model partition function can be reinterpreted as a sum over 12d geometries (Brinkmann metrics with varying $R_{uu}$), analogous to how the BFSS matrix model sums over 11d geometries.

**Expected outcome:** A direct 12d interpretation of the IKKT model's non-perturbative dynamics, potentially connecting the matrix model sum over matrices to a sum over 12d Brinkmann geometries.

**Feasibility:** Easy to moderate (weeks to months). Step 1 is directly doable with the existing code. Steps 2-3 are more speculative.

**References:** Ishibashi:1996xs, Hartnoll:2024csr, Komatsu:2024bop, Ciceri:2025wpb, Banks:1996vh

## 6. Geometrizing the Eisenstein Series: $E_{3/2}$ from 12d Geometry

**Motivation:** Section 7 makes the sharp observation: "in 12d $\tau$ should not show up." The Eisenstein series $E_{3/2}(\tau,\bar\tau)$ multiplying $R^4$ should emerge from pure geometry in 12d, not be inserted by hand. The review suggests $\mathfrak{L}^{(3)} \propto f(\mathcal{G}_{MN}, \mathcal{R}_{MNPQ})$, where the modular function arises upon torus reduction.

**Concrete approach:**
1. Compute $\mathcal{R}_{MNPQ}\mathcal{R}^{MNPQ}$ for the 12d metric ansatz $\mathcal{G}_{MN} = g_{mn} \oplus M_{ij}(\tau)$, systematically cataloguing which components involve $\tau$.
2. Look for 12d curvature invariants (contractions of $\mathcal{R}^4$ with the 12d metric) that, upon torus reduction, produce $\tau$-dependent prefactors.
3. Check whether the Eisenstein series $E_{3/2}(\tau,\bar\tau) = \sum_{(m,n)\neq(0,0)} \tau_2^{3/2}/|m+n\tau|^3$ can arise as a one-loop determinant of the 12d Laplacian on $T^2$ — this connects to the Schwinger calculation in direction 1.

**Expected outcome:** An explicit 12d invariant whose torus reduction produces $E_{3/2} \cdot R^4$, or a no-go argument showing this is impossible with purely geometric data.

**Feasibility:** Moderate (months). Step 1 can be done with the `src/sugra/` tools (extend to compute Kretschner scalar). Step 3 is standard spectral geometry but requires care with regularization.

**References:** Minasian:2015bxa, Green:1997as, Green:1997tv, Obers:1999um (on Eisenstein series and string dualities)

## 7. AdS$_5 \times S^5$ from 12d and the Wilson Loop Puzzle

**Motivation:** Section 8 notes that M2 brane one-loop computations in $AdS_4 \times S^7$ reproduce subleading $1/N$ corrections to Wilson loops in ABJM (Giombi:2023vzu, Tseytlin:2024euk). The review asks: is there an analogue for $AdS_5 \times S^5$? The D3 brane plays the role of the M2, but the 12d uplift of $AdS_5 \times S^5$ is needed to frame the question.

**Concrete approach:**
1. Uplift the $AdS_5 \times S^5$ solution to 12d using the metric ansatz: $\mathcal{G}_{MN} = g_{mn}^{AdS_5 \times S^5} \oplus M_{ij}(\tau_0)$ with constant $\tau_0$. The result is $AdS_5 \times S^5 \times T^2$.
2. Consider a "12d 3-brane" (the hypothetical uplift of the D3) wrapping a circle at the boundary of $AdS_5$, with the two extra directions wrapped on $T^2$. Compute the classical action.
3. Investigate whether a one-loop computation of the 3-brane worldvolume effective action in this background reproduces the known $1/N$ corrections to the $\frac{1}{2}$-BPS Wilson loop in $\mathcal{N}=4$ SYM.

**Expected outcome:** Either a successful reproduction of subleading Wilson loop corrections from a 12d perspective (paralleling the M2/ABJM story), or identification of the precise obstruction (likely related to the D3 embedding problem of direction 4).

**Feasibility:** Hard (6-12 months). This requires both solving the D3 uplift problem and performing a one-loop supergravity calculation on a curved background.

**References:** Giombi:2023vzu, Tseytlin:2024euk, Drukker-Gross-Tseytlin on Wilson loops
