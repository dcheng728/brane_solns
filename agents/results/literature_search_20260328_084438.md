### Topic 1: Self-dual Form Fields — Democratic Formulation and Sen's Action
*(Relevant to Section 6: 12d effective action and 5-form self-duality)*

**1. arXiv:2207.00626** — Karapet Mkrtchyan and Fridrich Valach, *"Democratic actions for type II supergravities"* (2022, updated 2023), published in Phys. Rev. Lett. / JHEP

- Presents a universal democratic Lagrangian for the bosonic sector of 10d type II supergravities, treating electric and magnetic RR potentials on equal footing. For type IIB, the 5-form self-duality equation is derived from the Lagrangian itself rather than imposed by hand. Also provides a formulation with manifest SL(2,ℝ) symmetry.
- **Relevance:** Directly supports Section 6's treatment of the self-dual 5-form. The 12d Hodge duality implementation described in the review is a complementary approach to the self-duality problem that this paper solves differently. A citation would allow comparison of the two approaches.
- **Assessment:** Extends and complements the review's approach.

**2. arXiv:2401.00549** — Jose Juan Fernandez-Melgarejo, Giacomo Giorgi, Carmen Gomez-Fayren, Tomas Ortin, Matteo Zatti, *"Democratic actions with scalar fields: symmetric sigma models, supergravity actions and the effective theory of the type IIB superstring"* (2023/2024)

- Solves how to dualize scalar fields into (d-2)-form potentials in democratic pseudoactions while preserving global symmetries (relevant for the SL(2,ℝ) coset of the axio-dilaton). Proposes a democratic pseudoaction for the full bosonic N=2B, d=10 supergravity with two 0-forms, two 2-forms, one 4-form, two 6-forms, three 8-forms, and manifest SL(2,R) invariance.
- **Relevance:** Section 6 (12d-covariant embedding) and Section 3–4 (axio-dilaton sector). The manifest SL(2,R) formulation directly connects to the modular properties discussed throughout.
- **Assessment:** Extends the review's framework.

**3. arXiv:2307.04748** — Chris Hull, *"Covariant Action for Self-Dual p-Form Gauge Fields in General Spacetimes"* (2023), JHEP

- Generalises Sen's action for self-dual p-forms to be covariant on any spacetime by introducing a second dynamical metric alongside the physical metric. Eliminates the need for a fixed Minkowski reference metric in the original Sen construction.
- **Relevance:** Section 6 uses self-duality conditions; Hull's covariantised Sen action is the state-of-the-art approach to the self-dual 5-form of type IIB. Direct relevance to any discussion of the democratic formulation in the 12d framework.
- **Assessment:** Extends and improves a formalism discussed in the review.

**4. arXiv:2501.10566** — Chris Hull, *"Coupling Self-Dual p-Form Gauge Fields to Self-Dual Branes"* (2025)

- Establishes consistent coupling of self-dual p-form gauge fields (using the two-metric Sen formalism) to branes via generalised Dirac branes, introducing generalised symmetries from Dirac brane deformations.
- **Relevance:** Relevant to both the D3 brane worldvolume discussion (Section 5) and the 5-form coupling in Section 6. The D3 brane couples to a self-dual 5-form, so consistent brane couplings in this formalism matter.
- **Assessment:** Extends the formalism relevant to the review.

**5. arXiv:2506.07219** — Pichet Vanichchapongjaroen, *"Duality-symmetric D3-brane action with twisted self-dual 2-form doublet"* (June 2025)

- Constructs a non-linear SL(2,ℝ) duality-symmetric D3-brane action using the Sen formalism, with a twisted self-dual 2-form doublet (the worldvolume gauge field doublet). Preserves diffeomorphism and kappa symmetry, and shows physical/unphysical sector decoupling.
- **Relevance:** Very directly relevant to Section 5 (D3 brane and SL(2,Z) electromagnetic duality). This is a modern, covariant implementation of exactly the worldvolume SL(2,R) symmetry the review identifies as key to the 12d connection.
- **Assessment:** Strongly supports and extends the D3 brane section.

**6. arXiv:2404.05380** — Sujiphat Janaun, Anajak Phonchantuek, Pichet Vanichchapongjaroen, *"Nonlinear chiral forms in the Sen formulation"* (2024)

- Studies nonlinear chiral (2p)-forms in 4p+2 dimensions in the Sen formulation, including decoupling at Hamiltonian and Lagrangian level, and shows that the M5-brane action separates into physical and unphysical components.
- **Relevance:** Relevant to Section 6; the 6-form / M5-brane self-duality story is the type IIA analogue of the type IIB 5-form problem.
- **Assessment:** Moderate relevance, useful for completeness.

### Topic 2: IKKT Matrix Model — New Holographic Results
*(Relevant to the D(-1)/pp-wave section and the Outlook)*

**7. arXiv:2503.08771** — Franz Ciceri and Henning Samtleben, *"Holography for the IKKT matrix model"* (March 2025)

- Identifies gauge-invariant operators in the lowest BPS multiplet of the IKKT model with Kaluza-Klein fluctuations of Euclidean IIB supergravity on the D(-1) instanton background. Constructs a 1d maximal supergravity with SO(10) encoding the full non-linear dynamics. Half-supersymmetric solutions are uplifted to both IIB supergravity and **12-dimensional pp-waves**.
- **Relevance:** Directly relevant to the D(-1)/pp-wave story (Introduction and relevant section). The uplift to 12d pp-waves is a concrete realisation of the review's claim that the D(-1) brane has a 12d interpretation. This is a *different* paper from the already-cited arXiv:2511.23111 (which was a companion proceeding).
- **Assessment:** Strongly supports the review's D(-1)/pp-wave claims with new holographic evidence.

**8. arXiv:2411.18678** — Shota Komatsu, Adrien Martina, João Penedones, Antoine Vuignier, Xiang Zhao, *"Einstein gravity from a matrix integral — Part II"* (November 2024, revised July 2025)

- Uses supersymmetric localisation to compute the partition function and protected correlators of the polarised IKKT model; recovers the electrostatic problem defining the gravity dual. Finds the original IKKT model differs from the polarised IKKT in the vanishing-mass limit.
- **Relevance:** Part I (arXiv:2410.18173) is already cited as `Komatsu:2024bop`. Part II should be cited alongside it for completeness.
- **Assessment:** Companion to an already-cited paper; recommend adding.

**9. arXiv:2504.06481** — Sean A. Hartnoll and Jun Liu, *"Statistical Physics of the Polarised IKKT Matrix Model"* (April 2025)

- Analyzes the D-instanton matrix model in background 3-form flux; finds a first-order phase transition at a critical Ω²N, with competing saddle points from a polarised spherical D1-brane and a spherical (p,q) fivebrane.
- **Relevance:** Follow-up to the already-cited `Hartnoll:2024csr` (arXiv:2409.18706). The (p,q) fivebrane competing saddle is directly relevant to the SL(2,Z) story.
- **Assessment:** Extends an already-cited paper; recommend adding.

### Topic 3: D-instantons and Non-perturbative Type IIB
*(Relevant to Section on D(-1) brane and SL(2,Z))*

**10. arXiv:2205.00609** — Nathan B. Agmon, Bruno Balthazar, Minjae Cho, Victor A. Rodriguez, and Xi Yin, *"D-instanton Effects in Type IIB String Theory"* (2022), JHEP

- Computes D-instanton contributions to supergraviton scattering amplitudes beyond leading non-perturbative order, including one-D-instanton effects on maximal R-symmetry violating amplitudes and D/anti-D instanton pair effects. Verifies S-duality predictions and uncovers new amplitude structures.
- **Relevance:** Relevant to the D(-1) brane interpretation and the non-perturbative completion of the type IIB S-matrix. Provides modern verification of S-duality at the amplitude level.
- **Assessment:** Supports the S-duality / D(-1) discussion.

### Topic 4: Higher-Derivative Corrections and Modular Properties
*(Relevant to Section 6 and the Outlook)*

**11. arXiv:2203.13021** — Daniele Dorigoni, Michael B. Green, Congkao Wen, *"The SAGEX Review on Scattering Amplitudes, Chapter 10: Selected topics on modular covariance of type IIB string amplitudes and their N=4 SYM duals"* (2022), J. Phys. A

- Reviews the interplay of supersymmetry and modular covariance for type IIB scattering amplitudes, exact expressions for BPS interactions, integrated correlators in N=4 SYM from supersymmetric localisation, and modular graph functions from genus-one amplitudes.
- **Relevance:** Reviews exactly the SL(2,Z)-covariant structure of higher-derivative corrections discussed in the Outlook. Provides the backdrop for the non-holomorphic Eisenstein series mentioned there.
- **Assessment:** Review article; good general reference for the modular structure section.

**12. arXiv:2501.03996** — Daniele Dorigoni, Michael B. Green, Congkao Wen, *"Modular Features of Superstring Scattering Amplitudes: Generalised Eisenstein Series and Theta Lifts"* (submitted August 2025)

- Proves that coefficients in the large-N expansion of integrated N=4 SYM correlators and two-loop modular graph functions admit a unified description as 4d lattice sums (theta lifts of local Maass functions). The generalised Eisenstein series — novel modular functions first seen in type IIB graviton amplitudes — are central.
- **Relevance:** The Outlook explicitly mentions "non-holomorphic Eisenstein series in higher-derivative couplings" as having a potential 12d interpretation. This paper is the current frontier on exactly that structure.
- **Assessment:** Directly relevant to the Outlook's discussion of modular completions and their 12d interpretation.

**13. arXiv:2210.14038** — Daniele Dorigoni, Michael B. Green, Congkao Wen, Haitian Xie, *"Modular-invariant large-N completion of an integrated correlator in N=4 SYM"* (2022)

- Uses supersymmetric localisation to derive a duality-invariant non-perturbative completion of the large-N expansion involving novel non-holomorphic modular functions that behave as (p,q)-string instanton contributions.
- **Relevance:** The (p,q) instanton contributions and their modular invariant resummation are directly related to the SL(2,Z)-covariant structure of type IIB amplitudes discussed throughout the review. The large-N AdS/CFT connection is also relevant to the Outlook.
- **Assessment:** Supports and extends discussions of SL(2,Z) in both the effective action section and the Outlook.
