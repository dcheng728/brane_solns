## 1. The 5-Point Amplitude Test: $\varepsilon_{12}\varepsilon_{12}\mathcal{R}^4$ from 12d

**Motivation:** Section 7 identifies this as the single most important test. The proposed 12d effective action $\mathfrak{L}^{(3)} \propto E_{3/2}(\mathfrak{t}_8\mathfrak{t}_8 + \varepsilon_{12}\varepsilon_{12})\mathcal{R}^4$ of [Minasian-Liu 2015](https://arxiv.org/abs/1304.3137) was shown to be inconsistent at 5-point by [Liu-ValeixoBento 2025](https://arxiv.org/abs/2512.10746). The $\epsilon_{10}\epsilon_{10}R^4$ term vanishes at 4-point but is *intrinsically* 10-dimensional at 5-point — it's the cleanest probe of whether 12d really knows about 10d physics.

**Concrete approach:**
1. Compute the 5-graviton KK-loop amplitude using the Schwinger proper-time formula (Section 7.3, eq. analogous to the 4-point formula) for KK modes on $T^2$. The 5-point Schwinger formula is given in [Liu-Minasian, Green-Gutperle-Vanhove].
2. Expand in $\alpha'$ and isolate the $\epsilon_{10}\epsilon_{10}R^4$ contact term.
3. Compare against the known type IIB 5-point amplitude at genus 1 with D(-1) backgrounds.

**Expected outcome:** Either (a) the KK-loop on $T^2$ reproduces the 10d $\epsilon_{10}\epsilon_{10}R^4$ term, validating the 12d$\to$10d correspondence at 5-point, or (b) it doesn't, sharply delineating where the 12d interpretation breaks down.

**Feasibility:** Moderate (months). The 5-point Schwinger integrals are technically demanding but well-defined. Symbolic/numerical tools in `src/sugra/` could be extended to handle the kinematic tensor contractions.

**References:**
- [Minasian, Liu (2015)](https://cea.hal.science/cea-01310353/document) — original 12d R^4 proposal
- [Liu, ValeixoBento (2025)](https://arxiv.org/abs/2512.10746) — 5-point inconsistency
- [Green, Gutperle, Vanhove (1999)](https://arxiv.org/abs/hep-th/9910056) — 5-point Schwinger formulas
- [Liu, Minasian (2013)](https://arxiv.org/abs/1304.3137) — higher-derivative couplings and dualities

## 2. Sen's Action for the 12d Self-Dual 5-Form Problem

**Motivation:** Section 6 shows that the 10d self-duality $F_5 = *_{10}F_5$ lifts to a 12d Hodge duality $\mathcal{F}_7 = *_{12}\mathcal{F}_5$. But the composite $\tilde{F}_5$ cannot be sensibly constructed in 12d (no pair of potential+strength summing to rank 5). The action (6.12) treats $\mathcal{F}_7$ as non-independent, related to $\mathcal{C}_4$ by Hodge duality — this is precisely the kind of problem Sen's action was designed to solve.

**Concrete approach:**
1. Apply Sen's covariant action formalism (which introduces an auxiliary free field) directly to the 12d system with the pair $(\mathcal{C}_4, \mathcal{C}_6)$ subject to $d\mathcal{C}_6 = *_{12}d\mathcal{C}_4$.
2. Verify that reducing on $T^2$ with the metric ansatz (6.1) reproduces the type IIB action with self-dual $F_5$.
3. Check whether the Sen auxiliary field acquires a 10d interpretation (perhaps related to the PST scalar).

**Expected outcome:** A manifestly covariant 12d action from which both the self-duality condition and the Chern-Simons coupling $\mathcal{C}_4 \wedge \mathcal{F}_4 \wedge \mathcal{F}_4$ follow from variation. This would solve the "no action principle" problem for the 12d repackaging.

**Feasibility:** Moderate (months). Sen's formalism is well-developed ([Sen 2019, arXiv:1903.12196](https://arxiv.org/abs/1903.12196); [Chakrabarti 2024](https://onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/full/10.1002/prop.202400023) for T-duality extensions). The key new element is applying it in 12d with the specific torus fibration structure.

**References:**
- [Sen (2019)](https://arxiv.org/abs/1903.12196) — Self-dual forms: action, Hamiltonian and compactification
- [Chakrabarti (2024)](https://onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/full/10.1002/prop.202400023) — T-duality for self-dual fields
- [Pasti, Sorokin, Tonin (1997)](https://arxiv.org/abs/hep-th/9611100) — PST formalism

## 3. The D3 Brane as a 12d 3-Brane: Resolving the Embedding Problem

**Motivation:** Section 5 identifies a tantalizing gap: the D3 has 2 on-shell bosonic worldvolume degrees of freedom (from a gauge field $A_m$), enough for 12d embedding coordinates, but they arise from a *gauge field*, not scalars. The M2$\to$D2 story works perfectly (worldvolume scalar $\to$ 11th coordinate), but the D3 analog is blocked. Meanwhile, the D3's SL(2,$\mathbb{Z}$) electromagnetic duality maps directly to bulk S-duality — it *should* be the 12d fundamental object.

**Concrete approach:**
1. Use the recently constructed [duality-symmetric D3-brane action with twisted self-dual 2-form doublet](https://arxiv.org/abs/2506.07219) which packages the gauge field and its dual into an SL(2,$\mathbb{Z}$)-covariant doublet.
2. In the doubled formulation, the worldvolume fields naturally pair into a 2-form doublet $(F, \tilde{F})$. Investigate whether imposing a 12d-covariant constraint (analogous to the self-duality constraint) reduces the degrees of freedom while preserving 12d embedding.
3. Alternatively, explore whether the gauge field can be dualized to a 0-form only after coupling to the bulk $\mathcal{M}_{ij}$ torus modulus, giving a *field-dependent* embedding coordinate.

**Expected outcome:** An action for a 3-brane in 12d that (a) reduces to the D3 DBI+WZ action on $T^2$ reduction, and (b) makes SL(2,$\mathbb{Z}$) manifest as a geometric symmetry of the 12d target space.

**Feasibility:** Hard (months to year). The conceptual obstruction is genuine, but the new duality-symmetric formulations give fresh tools.

**References:**
- [Tseytlin (1996)](https://arxiv.org/abs/hep-th/9602064) — D3 worldvolume SL(2,$\mathbb{Z}$)
- [arXiv:2506.07219](https://arxiv.org/abs/2506.07219) — Duality-symmetric D3 action
- [Schmidhuber (1996)](https://arxiv.org/abs/hep-th/9601003) — D2 $\to$ M2 via worldvolume duality

## 4. Geometrizing the Eisenstein Series: $E_{3/2}$ from 12d Geometry

**Motivation:** Section 7.2 makes a sharp observation: in a genuine 12d theory, $\tau$ should not appear as an independent field — it is part of the metric $\mathcal{G}_{MN}$. The current ansatz $E_{3/2}(\tau,\bar\tau) \cdot \mathcal{R}^4$ puts modular invariance in *by hand*. A truly 12d effective action should be $f(\mathcal{G}_{MN}, \mathcal{R}_{MNPQ})$, from which $E_{3/2}$ *emerges* upon torus reduction.

**Concrete approach:**
1. Study the spectral theory of the Laplacian on $T^2$ with modulus $\tau$. The Eisenstein series $E_{3/2}$ is an eigenfunction: $\Delta_\tau E_{3/2} = \frac{3}{4}E_{3/2}$. Ask whether this eigenvalue equation has a 12d geometric interpretation, e.g. as a condition on 12d curvature invariants integrated over the fiber.
2. Consider one-loop determinants of the 12d Laplacian on $T^2$: $\log\det(-\nabla^2_{12})|_{T^2}$ naturally produces Eisenstein series via zeta-function regularization. Investigate whether $\mathcal{R}^4 \cdot \log\det(-\nabla^2_{12})|_{T^2}$ reproduces the correct 10d effective action.
3. Compare with how the type IIA $R^4$ coefficient ($E_{3/2}$ of the $T^2$ from 11d on $S^1$) arises from the 11d one-loop amplitude.

**Expected outcome:** A 12d functional whose torus reduction yields $E_{3/2} \cdot R^4$ without putting in $\tau$-dependence by hand. This would make the 12d interpretation truly predictive.

**Feasibility:** Hard (year+). This is conceptually deep and connects to automorphic forms on reductive groups.

**References:**
- [Green, Miller, Vanhove (2011)](https://arxiv.org/abs/1111.2983) — Eisenstein series and string instantons
- [Green, Gutperle (1997)](https://arxiv.org/abs/hep-th/9701093) — D-instanton contributions to $R^4$
- [Pioline (2010)](https://arxiv.org/abs/1001.3647) — $R^4$ couplings and automorphic forms

## 5. AdS$_5$/CFT$_4$ from One-Loop D3 Brane: The 12d Angle

**Motivation:** Section 8 highlights that [Giombi-Tseytlin (2023)](https://arxiv.org/abs/2303.15207) reproduced subleading $1/N$ Wilson loop corrections in ABJM from a one-loop M2 brane in AdS$_4 \times S^7$. The natural question is whether an analogous computation exists for the D3 brane in AdS$_5 \times S^5$. The 12d perspective may be the missing ingredient: the M2 lives naturally in 11d; the D3 should live naturally in 12d.

**Concrete approach:**
1. Uplift the AdS$_5 \times S^5$ D3 brane embedding to 12d using the metric ansatz $\mathcal{G}_{MN} = g_{mn} \oplus \mathcal{M}_{ij}$, with $\tau = \text{const}$ (as for AdS$_5 \times S^5$). The 12d geometry becomes AdS$_5 \times S^5 \times T^2$.
2. Consider a "12d membrane" wrapping a circle in $T^2$ and the boundary of AdS$_5$, whose reduction gives the D3 wrapping the boundary.
3. Compute the one-loop partition function of fluctuations around this configuration, including all KK modes on $T^2$.
4. Compare with known $1/N$ corrections to the $\frac{1}{2}$-BPS Wilson loop in $\mathcal{N}=4$ SYM.

**Expected outcome:** A one-loop computation yielding the known subleading corrections to Wilson loops in $\mathcal{N}=4$ SYM, analogous to what Giombi-Tseytlin achieved for ABJM.

**Feasibility:** Moderate to hard (6-12 months). The technology exists from the M2 computation; the main challenge is the self-dual field on the D3 worldvolume and the role of $T^2$ KK modes.

**References:**
- [Giombi, Tseytlin (2023)](https://arxiv.org/abs/2303.15207) — Wilson loops and quantum M2 brane
- [Giombi, Kurlyand, Tseytlin (2024)](https://link.springer.com/article/10.1007/JHEP11(2024)056) — Non-planar ABJM from M2 branes
- [Beccaria, Tseytlin (2023)](https://link.springer.com/article/10.1134/S1063776123120154) — Quantum supermembranes and AdS/CFT

## 6. U(1)-Violating Terms and the 12d Obstruction

**Motivation:** Section 7.2 identifies U(1)-violating terms at 5-point as the "primary obstruction" to finding 12d uplifts of effective actions. Type IIB amplitudes with $n > 4$ external states can violate the U(1) R-symmetry by $q_U = 2(n-4)$ units. The coefficients are modular forms of weight $(w, -w)$. In 12d, U(1) is part of the Lorentz group SO(1,1) on $T^2$ — so U(1) violation must have a 12d geometric origin.

**Concrete approach:**
1. Classify U(1)-violating terms at 5-point using the known results of [ValeixoBento et al. (2022)](https://arxiv.org/abs/2205.11530) and the recent [5-point axio-dilaton couplings](https://arxiv.org/abs/2507.07934).
2. In 12d, U(1) arises from $\Gamma_{10,11}$ (Section 4.4). U(1)-violation means the 12d amplitude has non-trivial dependence on the orientation of the torus cycle. Investigate whether non-geometric effects (e.g., stringy winding modes on $T^2$) can account for U(1)-violating corrections.
3. Check whether the U(1)-violating terms can be absorbed into a modified 12d$\to$10d reduction ansatz that includes off-diagonal metric components $\mathcal{G}_{m,10}$ and $\mathcal{G}_{m,11}$.

**Expected outcome:** Either a 12d mechanism that generates U(1)-violating terms (resolving the obstruction), or a sharp no-go theorem delineating what part of the type IIB effective action admits a 12d uplift.

**Feasibility:** Moderate to hard (6-12 months).

**References:**
- [ValeixoBento et al. (2022)](https://arxiv.org/abs/2205.11530) — Type IIB at eight derivatives
- [arXiv:2507.07934](https://arxiv.org/abs/2507.07934) — Five-point axio-dilaton couplings
- [D'Hoker, Green, Pioline (2019)](https://link.springer.com/article/10.1007/JHEP06(2019)087) — Modular forms and SL(2,$\mathbb{Z}$)-covariance
